Geometry

Angles

are measures of turns between two lines.

Types of angles include acute (less than 90°), right (exactly 90°), and obtuse (greater than 90°).

Triangles

are shapes with three sides. The sum of the interior angles of any triangle is 180°.

Types of triangles include equilateral (all sides equal), isosceles (two sides equal), and scalene (all sides different).

Quadrilaterals

shapes such as squares, rectangles, and parallelograms.

Polygons

hapes with many sides, like pentagons, hexagons, and octagons.

Circles

have special measurements.

The radius is the distance from the center to the edge.

The diameter is the distance across the circle and equals two times the radius.

The circumference is the distance around the circle and equals 2πr.

The area of a circle equals π multiplied by the radius squared.
